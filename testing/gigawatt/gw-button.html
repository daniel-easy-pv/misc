Styles

Buttons have four styles: 'Primary', 'Secondary', 'Tertiary', and 'Link'. The 'Primary' style is recommended for the principal Call to Action (CTA) on a page, and typically, only one 'Primary' button should be employed per page. 'Secondary' CTAs serve when a less pivotal action is taken or as the counterpart to a 'Primary' button, such as Submit (Primary) and cancel (Secondary). 'Tertiary' buttons are suitable for less critical functions that users actively seek or in scenarios where multiple buttons are used in conjunction. 'Link' buttons are used for applications where a hyperlink is embedded within the text.

                                    Button

Icons

Icons may be incorporated into buttons, either alongside text or on their own. They should only be used when they contribute the user's comprehension of the button's function. Buttons consisting solely of icons should be employed sparsely and exclusively when the icon clearly conveys the intended action; otherwise, text should accompany the icon.

                                    Button

Sizing

Buttons are categorized into three sizes: Default, Small, and Large. Default is recommended for most use cases, while Small is suitable for compact spaces, particularly on mobile platforms. If using small buttons, care should be taken to ensure visibility and accessibility, especially when incorporating icons. Large is ideal for spacious pages or prominent calls to action (CTAs).
